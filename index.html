Waterville Valley is a city in the White Mountains region of New Hampshire. The city was incorporated in 1829, and as of the 2020 census, the population was 508 (49% female and 51% male). The median age was 45 years old.

There were approximately 121 households, of which 27% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 46% were married couples living together and 35% were made up of individuals. The average family size was 2.72 and the average household size was 2.12. The population density was 4.0 people per square mile.

The medium houshold income of Waterville Valley, NH residents was $89,276 and the per capita income was $73,020. The number grew significantly since 2020. The town has two schools, the Waterville Valley Elementary School (called the "Little Red School") which has 41 students enrolled in K-8th grade, as well as the Waterville Valley Academy (WVA) which has 160 students.

In terms of climate, Waterville warm-summer humid continental climate. The average temperature in the winter and summer is about 19 and 60 degrees fahrenheit respectively On average, there is 122" of snow per year and 194 sunny days per year.

In terms of geography, the town has a total area of 64.4 miles, with the center of the town draining into the Mad River, which flows southwest to the Pemigewasset River (a part of the larger Merrimack River watershed). The highest point in Waterville Valley is the North Peak of Mount Tripyramid (4,180 feet above sea level). Other 4,000 footer mountains in Waterville Valley include Mount Tecumseh (4,003 feet), North Peak Tripyramid (4,180 feet), Middle Peak Tripyramid (4,140 feet), South Peak Tripyramid (4,100 feet), Mt. Osceola (4,340 feet), and Mt. Osceola East (4,156 feet).
